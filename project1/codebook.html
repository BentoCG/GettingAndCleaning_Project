Features on this database were obtained from the accelerometer ('Acc') and gyroscope('Gyro') 3-axial raw signals. Prefix 't' stands for time domain signals, while 'f' indicates frequency domain signals. 'Mag' denotes the magnitude of these XYZ signals according to the Euclidean norm. Every possible combination of this. The acceleration signal was separated between 'Body' and 'Gravity', while Gyroscope signals are only available for 'Body'. Columns 1 and 2 stand for the Subject ID and the activity type.

This dataset is restricted to the mean and std of every possible variable combination explained

above, adding up a total of 66 variables.

Three-axial Variables

Signals from all axes are separated in different columns (i.e. There is a 'tBodyAcc-X', tBodyAcc-Y',

and a 'tBodyAcc-Z' column.) For each axis, there is a mean and std measure.
